Hello!

I'm a big fan of minimalist and performant web architecture.

Although there are many warring thoughts on what minimalist websites should be, I tend to optimize for load times while maintaining styling flexibility

There are a few of examples of these styling flexible websites that can also be used as a guide to create your own, but I worry that these websites are also too extreme with their philosophies.

Websites following strict philosophies might have minimal footprint, but miss out on interactable frontends and analytics backends that create a good experience for users in the front and developers in the back.

Conversely, websites that tend to forgo any limitations tend to create experiences where load time are insanely long. (~10 Seconds for Facebook)

But that doesn't have to be the case, sitewide, these pages take a bit under 250kb of resources for the 3 pages. And we still get pages that load instantly, have a bit of character and have an analytics engine running in the background.

Pages like these can be created using smarter build tools, stronger web architectural education and a desire to make the web a better place.

Performance and flexibility do not have to be a choice on the internet.
